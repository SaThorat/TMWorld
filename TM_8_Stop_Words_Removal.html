Stop Word Removal

1. What are Stop Words?

Stop words are actually the most commonly used words in any language (like articles, prepositions, pronouns, conjunctions, etc.). Generally these words does not add much information to the text. So a search engine has been programmed to ignore them while searching and retrieving.

Examples:

In English, stop words are “the”, “a”, “an”, “so”, “what”.

2. Why do we remove stop words?
  1. Reduce the size of dataset
  2. Give more focus on important information
  3. Reduce training time and increase search performance
  4. Does not show negative consequences on trained model
  5. Does not add much information to text
3. Stop word removal

Removal of stop words is depending upon the task that we are going to perform. It is not necessary to remove stop words for all tasks.

  • Remove stop words in tasks like
    • Text Classification – E.g. Spam Filtering, Language Classification, Genre Classification
    • Caption Generation
    • Auto-Tag Generation
  • Avoid removing stop words in following tasks
    • Machine Translation
    • Language Modelling
    • Text Summarization
    • Question-Answering problems
