Named Entity Recognition

1. What is Named Entity Recognition?

Named-entity recognition (NER) is a subtask of information extraction that seeks to locate and classify named entities mentioned in unstructured text into pre-defined categories such as person names, organizations, locations, medical codes, time expressions, quantities, percentages, etc
NER also known as (named) entity identification, entity chunking, and entity extraction).[4]

Named-entity recognition is two-step process :

1. Detection of the names:
Detecting an entity or keyword from the given input text. The entity can be a word or a group of words

2. Classification of the names :
Creation of entity categories such as person, Organization, Time, date, Location, percentage, money etc. and you can also create your own categories.

Good training data is essential for a model to categorize detected entities into a relevant predetermined entity. Therefore, it’s vital to train the model correctly on suitable data. [3]

2. What are the Methods of Named Entity Recognition?

Methods of Named Entity Recognition:

1. Dictionary-based :

In this approach, a dictionary containing vocabulary is used. Basic string-matching algorithms check whether the entity is present in the given text against the items in the vocabulary

2. Rule-based

In this method, a predefined set of rules for information extraction is used which are pattern-based and context-based. Pattern-based rule uses the morphological pattern of the words while context-based uses the context of the word given in the text document.

3. Machine learning-based

It is a statistical-based model that tries to make a feature-based representation of the observed data. It can recognize an existing entity name even with small spelling variations.
Two Phases:
1. Train the ML model on annotated Documents
2. Trained model is used to annotate the raw documents

4. Deep learning-based

Deep learning based NER possesses the ability to bring words together, enhancing its comprehension of the semantic and syntactic connections among different words.
Furthermore, it has the capacity to automatically analyze words that pertain to specific topics and possess a higher level of meaning. [2]

3. What are the steps involved in algorithm of Named Entity Recognition?

A typically named entity recognition NLP model consists of several subtasks, including:
1. Tokenization:Tokenization breaks text into individual tokens (usually words or punctuation marks).
2. Part-of-speech tagging: Labelling each token with its corresponding part of speech (e.g., noun, verb, adjective, etc.).
3. Chunking: Group tokens into "chunks" based on their part-of-speech tags.
4. Name entity recognition: Identifying named entities and classifying them into predefined categories.
5. Entity disambiguation: The process of determining the correct meaning of a named entity, especially when multiple entities with the same name are present in the text. [7]

Example:

Sentence :
"Mark Zuckerberg founded Facebook in 2004 in Menlo Park, California”
1. Tokenize the sentence :
['Mark', 'Zuckerberg', 'founded', 'Facebook', 'in', '2004', 'in', 'Menlo', 'Park', ',', 'California', '.']
2. Part-of-specch Tagging:
['NNP', 'NNP', 'VBD', 'NNP', 'IN', 'CD', 'IN', 'NNP', 'NNP', ',', 'NNP', '.']
3. Chunking:
[('Mark', 'NNP'), ('Zuckerberg', 'NNP'), ('founded', 'VBD'), ('Facebook', 'NNP'), ('in', 'IN'), ('2004', 'CD'), ('in', 'IN'), ('Menlo', 'NNP'), ('Park', 'NNP'), (',', ','), ('California', 'NNP'), ('.', '.')]
4. Named Entity Recognition:
{ ('Mark Zuckerberg', 'PERSON'), ('2004', 'DATE'), ('Menlo Park', 'Location'), ('California', 'Location')}
5. Entity disambiguation :
If multiple people have the name "Mark Zuckerberg", we must determine which one is being referred to in the text.

What are the Benefits and Challenges of NER?
Named entity recognition provides a range of advantages when used appropriately:

  1. Automates the information extraction of large amounts of data.
  2. Analyzes key information in unstructured text.
  3. Eliminates human error in analysis.
  4. Frees up time for employees to perform other tasks.
  5. Improves the precision of NLP tasks and processes.

NER also comes with its own range of challenges:

  1. Difficulty in analyzing lexical ambiguities, semantics and evolving usages of language in text.
  2. Runs into problems with spelling variations.
  3. Doesn't know all foreign words.
  4. Can have issues with spoken word text, such as telephone conversations.
  5. Leads to many state-of-the-art NER models reporting limited performance measures.
  6. Can require large volumes of training data or a lot of human intervention.
  7. Runs the risk of exhibiting bias in outcomes if the machine learning algorithm contains underlying biases.[1]

What are the named entity recognition applications?

NER is widely used in various NLP tasks. These include:

  1. Efficient search algorithms
  2. Content recommendations
  3. Customer support
  4. Healthcare
  5. Summarizing resumes
  6. Content classification for news channels.[5]
