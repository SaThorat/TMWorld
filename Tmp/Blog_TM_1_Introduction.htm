What is Text Mining and Why it is Important?

1.  Importance of Text Mining

Data present on Internet in the form of text is increasing day by day. Examples of text or unstructured data are News articles, Blogs, Log files, Various reports published by companies working in Government and Private sectors, Interaction among people on Social networks, Product reviews shared by the customers, Research papers, Patents, Legal judgements given by the Courts, etc. It is expected that by 2025 80% global data will be in unstructured form. Processing unstructured data to identify interesting patterns from it is always a challenging task for data analysts teams. Even though a huge amount of information is present in text format till date there is less success in processing such in efficient manner. To analyze and get structured information from the data generated by these applications we need some sort of mechanism or methods. The solution is text mining.

2.  What is Text Mining

Text Mining is process of transforming unstructured text data into structured form in order to identify meaningful patterns and insights from the data.

Text mining comprises various activities which help in converting text / unstructured data into structured data. Typically, it starts with pre-processing of data. Pre-processing involves cleaning of the data and transforming data into usable form. Some popular text mining techniques are : Information Extraction (IE), Information Retrieval (IR), Natural Language Processing (NLP), Machine Learning and Deep Learning.

Above text mining techniques are used to perform various text mining tasks. Examples of these tasks are Tokenization, Stemming, Feature selection, Feature extraction, Named Entity Recognition (NER), Part-of-Speech (PoS) tagging, Text annotation, Summarization, Sentiment analysis, Text categorization, etc.

3.  Applications of Text Mining

Text Mining is useful in numerous domains. Below we have listed few domains and potential use of text mining in these domains:

1.    Marketing and Customer Experience

By employing Text analytics companies can gain insights into how customers perceive their brand and products through various channels like customer feedback, online reviews and social media comments. Further, text analytics can be used to uncover the emerging trends and customer preferences by analyzing textual data like ecommerce review, social media posts

2.    Finance:

Text analytics can be used to assess the market using financial news and social media analysis, and based on those insights, a decision on a certain stock can be made. It is also useful tool for detecting potential fraudulent activities by analyzing textual data from customer communications and transactions.

3.    Legal AI

Text analytics algorithm are more efficient to categorize and organize the large volume of legal documents, making it easier for legal professionals to access relevant information quickly. It can assist in e-discovery by identifying and prioritizing relevant documents during the pre-trial phase of a legal case, thereby reducing time and costs associated with manual document review.

4.    HealthCare:

Text Analytics help to monitor and analyze patient reports and medical records to identify side effect related to drugs and medical devices. It can assist healthcare professionals to process medical literature and patient records to extract the relevant information which provide valuable insights.

5.    Education:

In education field, written assignments can be analyzed and utilized using text analytics to give automated grading and feedback. Also students feedback can be utilized using text analytics to summarizing and categorizing student feedback

6.    Human Resource:

Text analytics can automate the process of screening job applications by analyzing resumes and identifying candidates with the desired qualifications and experience. Text analytics can be used to analyze employee feedback, surveys, and communication to assess employee satisfaction and identify potential issues within the organization.
